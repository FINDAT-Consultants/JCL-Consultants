Defeating Fraud with Change & Knowledge Management

Simon John, FDA – Business Forensicist 🔍📈 | January 17, 2025

The Evolving Fraud Landscape

COVID‑19 relief fund abuses and rising global fraud schemes underscore the need for stronger defences.

Key Statistics

  • 20% of firms omit data analytics in procurement fraud detection (2024 study).
  • 75% of boards now receive regular fraud‑mitigation updates.
  • Billions lost globally in pandemic‑relief scams targeting SMEs.

Role of Change Management

Guiding people through technology transitions ensures adoption and impact.

Adoption Roadmaps

Structured plans reduce resistance to AI‑powered detection tools.

Culture of Agility

Employees embrace innovation when leaders model adaptability.

Upskilling

Training aligns skills with new forensic technologies and workflows.

Power of Knowledge Management

Institutionalising lessons ensures collective vigilance.

Key Mechanisms

  • Centralised incident repositories & playbooks.
  • Peer‑learning forums and post‑mortem workshops.
  • Real‑time dashboards tracking fraud KPIs.

Change + Knowledge = Synergy

Integrated disciplines accelerate fraud‑defence maturity.

Without Change Management

AI tools under‑utilised due to employee resistance.

Without Knowledge Management

Skills gap prevents fully leveraging technology benefits.

Integrated Approach

  • Embed fraud insights into onboarding programs.
  • Link change milestones with knowledge‑sharing goals.
  • Reward teams for innovation and transparency.

Forging Ahead

Fraud tactics evolve—so must our defences.

Next‑Gen Tools

Predictive analytics and machine learning flag anomalies in real time—provided organisations cultivate change‑ready cultures.

Human Intelligence

Knowledge networks spread best practices, ensuring tech deployments reflect on‑the‑ground realities.

Call to Action

Commit to continuous improvement—embrace change, share knowledge, and lead the charge against fraud.
