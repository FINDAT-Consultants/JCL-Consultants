Fostering a Greener Future in Zambia

Aligning with IFRS S1 & S2 to unlock sustainable growth and attract global capital—powered by the historic K217.1 billion 2025 budget.

Why IFRS S1 & IFRS S2 Matter

Transparent sustainability and climate‑related disclosures build investor confidence and support Zambia’s energy transition.

IFRS S1

Highlights sustainability risks & opportunities—vital for mining, agriculture, and renewable sectors.

IFRS S2

Mandates climate‑related metrics such as emissions, forging a path toward net‑zero goals.

Global Capital

Clear reporting standards make Zambian firms investable for ESG‑focused funds.

Zambia’s Record‑Breaking 2025 Budget

K217.1 billion with 80.2% financed by domestic revenue—fuel for sustainable development.

Growth Priorities

  • GDP growth target 6.6%
  • Focus sectors: Renewable Energy, Agriculture, Mining & Infrastructure
  • Projected surplus: K11.08 billion

Integrating IFRS S1 & S2 across these sectors will channel budget resources into transparent, impact‑driven projects.

Managing Green Investment Risks

Robust disclosures mitigate exposure and reassure investors.

Hydro‑Dependence

Droughts slash hydro output—renewables must reduce reliance by 15% to cut projected losses from $4 m to $3.4 m.

Currency Fluctuations

Transparent hedging disclosures instill confidence among foreign investors.

Policy Alignment

Failure to meet IFRS S2 metrics can trigger compliance penalties and financing delays.

Supply‑Chain Emissions

Scope 3 reporting highlights areas for reduction, ensuring resilience.

Forensic Accounting for Compliance

Assuring transparency in sustainability pledges.

Audit in Action

If a firm pledges a 20% emission cut over five years, forensic audits track quarterly performance and recommend corrective measures.

  • Data Analytics identify variances early.
  • Remediation plans redirect budget to renewable assets.
  • Continuous reporting meets IFRS S2 timeline.

Key Benefits for Zambia

Green Investment

IFRS alignment opens the door to ESG funds and climate finance.

Job Creation

Renewable projects generate skilled employment across provinces.

Community Impact

Local micro‑grids power schools & clinics—audits ensure funds reach projects.

Navigating the Future

With IFRS S1 & S2, Zambia can balance economic growth and sustainability—securing a resilient, low‑carbon future.

Call to Action

Private‑sector leaders must embed IFRS disclosures into strategy, collaborate with regulators, and invest in up‑skilling finance teams.

Government Role

Streamline green‑bond frameworks and offer tax incentives for verified emission‑reduction projects.

Key Next Steps

  • Launch nationwide AI‑powered sustainability data hubs.
  • Expand rural broadband to enable digital reporting.
  • Foster public‑private taskforces to align investment with IFRS metrics.
